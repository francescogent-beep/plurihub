# Using ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini Together Without Losing Your Mind

If you've been using AI tools for more than six months, you've probably stopped asking "which AI is best" and started asking "which AI is best for this specific thing." That is the right question.

ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are genuinely different tools. They have different strengths. Experienced AI users use all three, switching between them depending on what they need. The problem is that this creates a management nightmare — conversations scattered across three platforms, prompts being retyped into each one, no way to compare outputs without switching tabs constantly.

Here's how to make it work cleanly.

## What each model actually does better

This is a rough guide, not a law. Models update constantly and individual results vary. But these tendencies hold up across most use cases:

**ChatGPT (GPT-4o and o-series)** is the all-rounder. Strong at coding, structured tasks, following complex multi-step instructions. The widest ecosystem of plugins and integrations. Best default choice when you don't have a reason to choose something else.

**Claude (Claude 3.5 / 4)** tends to produce longer, more nuanced written output. Better than GPT at maintaining a consistent tone across a long document. Stronger at tasks that require genuine reasoning about ambiguity — ethical considerations, editorial judgment, complex analysis. Many writers and researchers prefer Claude for drafting and editing.

**Gemini** is deeply integrated with Google's ecosystem. If your workflow involves Google Docs, Gmail, YouTube, or Google Search context, Gemini has access to things the others don't. Also strong for multimodal tasks involving images and documents.

**Perplexity** is different from all three — it's a research tool first, with real-time web access and citations. Use it when you need current information or want to verify facts with sources, not when you need generative writing.

## The practical workflow

For most professional AI users, the workflow looks something like this:

- **Start a project in Claude or ChatGPT** — use whichever you trust for the type of thinking required.
- **Research phase with Perplexity** — when you need facts, current information, or sources.
- **Verification round with a second model** — paste your Claude output into ChatGPT (or vice versa) and ask "what's wrong with this?" The disagreements are often more useful than the original output.
- **Google-native tasks in Gemini** — anything involving your Google Workspace.

The compare-and-contrast step is particularly valuable. Two models will often disagree on strategic questions, editorial choices, or complex reasoning. That disagreement is signal. It tells you where to think harder.

## The problem with this workflow

You end up with conversations in four different places. You close a tab and lose a thread. You forget which model gave you the response you liked. You retype the same context briefing into each new conversation.

Over time, this friction makes the multi-model workflow feel more exhausting than it's worth, and people fall back to using just one model — losing the advantages of the others.
