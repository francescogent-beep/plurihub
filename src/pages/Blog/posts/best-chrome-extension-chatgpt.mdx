# The Best Chrome Extension for ChatGPT in 2026

If you use ChatGPT seriously — for work, research, writing, or anything that actually matters — you have probably run into the same problem. You had a brilliant conversation three days ago. You can't find it. You've scrolled through history, searched for keywords you remember typing, and opened six tabs that weren't the right one.

ChatGPT's built-in conversation history is organised by recency, not by project. If you use it daily, it becomes unusable within a week.

The extension that solves this is **PluriHub**.

## What PluriHub does

PluriHub is a free Chrome extension that adds a sidebar to your browser. When you're in a ChatGPT conversation you want to keep, you save it with one click. You give it a title. It appears in your sidebar, organised however you want.

That's it. No API keys. No subscription. No data leaving your browser.

The real power is in **Spaces** — project folders inside PluriHub. You can have a "Client Work" space, a "Research" space, a "Personal" space. Your saved ChatGPT conversations sit inside whichever space makes sense. When you need to find something, you open the space and find it in seconds — not by scrolling through 400 conversations sorted by time.

## Why it works with more than just ChatGPT

Here is where PluriHub separates itself from extensions built specifically for ChatGPT: it supports every major AI platform.

Claude, Gemini, Grok, Perplexity, DeepSeek, Mistral, Copilot, Meta AI, NotebookLM, Poe, Pi, OpenRouter. The same sidebar. The same folder system. One place for your entire AI workflow, regardless of which tool you used.

If you use ChatGPT for one thing and Claude for another — which most serious AI users do — you no longer need to keep track of which platform holds which conversation. It all goes into PluriHub.

## The Prompt Library

The other feature worth knowing about is the **Prompt Library**. If you have prompts you use repeatedly — a briefing template, a system prompt for a specific task, a way you like to ask for feedback on your writing — you save them in PluriHub and paste them into any AI tool in seconds.

No more retyping. No more keeping a notes file with your favourite prompts open in a separate window.

## Does it read my conversations?

No. PluriHub saves links to conversations, not the text inside them. When you save a ChatGPT conversation, PluriHub stores the URL. Your actual conversation content stays in ChatGPT's servers, exactly where it always was. PluriHub never reads, copies, or transmits your chat text.

## How to install it

PluriHub is free and available on the Chrome Web Store. Search for "PluriHub" or go to [plurihub.com](https://plurihub.com) and click "Add to Chrome."

It takes about 15 seconds to install. After that, you'll find it in Chrome's side panel — the icon on the right side of your browser window.
